Biography

Alexander is a web development engineer at Kapsch TrafficCom North America, a provider of intelligent transportation systems in several fields, including tolling and smart urban mobility. He contributes code that is written primarily in JavaScript, TypeScript, HTML, and CSS to Kapsch’s enterprise-level single-page applications, using Angular CLI for the front-end and node.js for the back-end.

Prior to joining the UI/UX team, he was a technical writer at Kapsch, where he coordinated throughput for five active projects, developed in-house training materials, and performed heavy copyedits, including rewriting ESL content. He was also the primary contributor to the technical writing team’s documentation development toolchain. He developed a VSTO Word Add-in, written in C#, that streamlines and automates document-control-related tasks, and he also created a document-tracking-friendly front-end for Kapsch's Jira.

Alexander has many interests and hobbies outside of work, including woodworking and writing fiction. He is currently working on his fourth novel, Forgive Me, Father, which he hopes to publish.
